DOG BITE

Dog bites can happen to anyone. Even the gentlest canines can bite! They may feel threatened, scared, or overprotective of someone they love. It can also happen in seconds.

If you get bitten by a dog, wash the area well, let it bleed out, and see your doctor immediately (within 6 to 8 hours of the bite). You need to take anti-rabies and anti-tetanus shots right away if you were bitten by an unknown dog or if your own dog’s rabies vaccines are not up to date. Even if you think the wound is minor, it’s best to consult a doctor, especially if you get bitten in the face or hand. Do remember that rabies can be fatal.
